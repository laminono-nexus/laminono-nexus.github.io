The Chronos Protocol

The Clock That Should Not Exist

Time Travel Thriller
Episode 1 Reading Time: ~4 minutes

Daniel noticed the silence first. Not the normal kind—the soft midnight quiet that settles gently around you. This silence felt wrong. Heavy. Like the world had stopped… and forgotten how to move again.

He stared at his phone. 03:17 AM. The time hadn't changed in over ten minutes. Daniel frowned. "Network issue," he muttered, rubbing his eyes. Another sleepless night. Another deadline chasing him into the early hours. But something wasn't right.

He slowly looked up. The ceiling fan above him had stopped mid-spin. Not slowing. Not turning. Frozen. Daniel stood up carefully, his heartbeat beginning to rise. "Hello?" he called out. No response. The air felt thick, almost difficult to breathe.

He walked to the window and pulled the curtain aside. Outside, the street was wrong. A car sat in the middle of the road. Its headlights were on, casting a pale glow across the pavement—but the car wasn't moving. The driver inside… wasn't moving either.

Daniel's chest tightened. "This has to be a dream…" He rushed outside, barefoot, the cold ground barely registering beneath him. Everything felt delayed, like he was walking through invisible water. He approached the car slowly. The driver, a middle-aged man, was frozen mid-expression—his mouth slightly open, his face twisted in annoyance. Daniel waved his hand in front of the man's face. Nothing. No blink. No breath.

A quiet sound cut through the silence. Tick. Daniel turned sharply. Behind him, standing at the edge of the street, was something that hadn't been there before. A tall, antique clock. Its wooden frame was dark and aged. The glass was slightly cracked. And its hands… were moving. Tick. Tick.

Daniel swallowed hard. Everything else in the world had stopped. But this clock was alive. He stepped closer, drawn by something he didn't understand. At the base of the clock, a small engraving caught his eye. C.P. — 03:17. His breath caught in his throat. "That's my time…"

The ticking grew louder. Sharper. More deliberate. Daniel hesitated. Every instinct told him to step back. To run. To pretend none of this was real. But curiosity pulled him forward. Slowly, he reached out his hand. His fingers trembled as they touched the glass.

The world snapped. Sound exploded back into existence. A car horn blared violently. Voices shouted in the distance. The ceiling fan roared back to life. Daniel stumbled backward, gasping. He was back in his apartment. He grabbed his phone. 03:18 AM. Only one minute had passed.

"No… that's not possible…" He rushed to the window again. Everything was normal. Cars moving. People walking. No frozen world. No clock. Daniel paced the room, running his hands through his hair. "I need sleep… this is just stress…"

His phone buzzed. Unknown sender. No number. A message appeared. "You touched it." "That means you're ready." "Welcome to The Chronos Protocol."

Daniel froze. "What protocol?" Another message appeared instantly. "Time is broken." "You can see what others cannot." "Check your door."

A knock followed. Soft. Precise. Daniel turned slowly toward the door. Another knock. "Who is it?" he asked. Silence. He stepped forward, his heart pounding louder with each step. He opened the door. No one was there. Only a small black device on the ground.

He picked it up. It felt cold. Too cold. The screen flickered to life. 03:17 AM. "That again…" The numbers began to glitch. 03:17 → 02:59 → 11:42 → 03:17. Faster. Faster. Then it stopped.

A message appeared on the screen. "Press it… if you want the truth." Daniel stared at it. His mind told him to drop it. Walk away. Forget everything. But something deeper pulled him in. "Just one press…" he whispered. His finger hovered above the screen. Then he tapped it.

Everything went dark. Far away… somewhere beyond time… A voice whispered: "Subject activated."
