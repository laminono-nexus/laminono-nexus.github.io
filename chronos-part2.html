The Chronos Protocol

The First Jump

Time Travel Thriller
Episode 2 Reading Time: ~4 minutes

Darkness didn't feel empty. It felt full. Like Daniel had stepped into a space that was waiting for him. He tried to move. Nothing happened. No ground beneath his feet. No air against his skin. Just a strange pressure, like being suspended inside something unseen.

Then came the sound. A low hum at first. Then voices. Whispers. Not one. Not two. Many. Daniel turned, but there was nothing to see. "Who's there?" His voice echoed, stretching farther than it should. A light flickered ahead. Small at first. Then growing. The moment it touched him— He was gone.

Daniel gasped and dropped to his knees. Concrete scraped his palms. Air rushed back into his lungs like he had been underwater. He looked up quickly. This was not his street. The buildings were the same—but different. Older. Faded. The paint on the walls was peeling. Some shops were closed, their signs broken or missing.

A bus passed by. Daniel froze. It looked outdated, like something from years ago. People walked past him. No one stopped. No one reacted. "Where am I…?" He stood slowly, trying to steady himself. Then he noticed something else. The street felt familiar. Too familiar. His eyes moved across the road… until they landed on a small shop at the corner. His breath caught. That shop had been demolished years ago. He remembered clearly. A fire. A collapse. It had been all over the news. But here it was. Standing. Whole. Untouched.

"This doesn't make sense…" A sudden chill ran down his spine. If the shop was still standing… Then this wasn't his time. Daniel stepped backward slowly. "No… no, no…" A loud crash echoed down the street. People began to shout. Daniel turned toward the sound. Smoke was rising from the direction of the shop. His chest tightened. He knew this moment. "This is the day it happened…" The fire. The collapse. The deaths.

Without thinking, Daniel started running. "Hey!" someone shouted as he pushed past. But he didn't stop. The shop was already burning. Flames climbed the walls, devouring everything in seconds. People stood at a distance, watching, shouting, panicking. Daniel's heart pounded violently. "I remember this… there were people inside…" A woman screamed. "My son! He's inside!"

Daniel froze. That didn't happen before. He didn't remember that. His mind raced. "Was that always there… or did something change?" The fire grew stronger. The building creaked dangerously. Daniel stepped forward. Then stopped. If this was the past… Then anything he did here could change everything. But if he did nothing… Someone might die.

The woman's screams cut through him. "Please! Somebody help him!" Daniel clenched his fists. "This isn't my problem…" But his feet didn't move. Another crack echoed from the building. Time was running out. "Damn it…" Before he could think any further— Daniel ran into the fire.

Heat slammed into him instantly. Smoke filled his lungs. His eyes burned. "Hello?!" he shouted. "Is anyone here?!" A faint cough answered him. Daniel followed the sound deeper inside. The ceiling groaned above him. Debris fell around him. Then he saw him. A small boy, trapped beneath a fallen shelf.

"Don't move!" Daniel said, rushing forward. The boy looked up, terrified. "You came…" Daniel didn't answer. He pushed the shelf aside with everything he had. "Can you stand?" The boy nodded weakly. "Good. Stay close." Another loud crack. The building was seconds from collapse. Daniel grabbed the boy's hand and ran. The exit was just ahead. Light breaking through the smoke.

Then— Everything froze. The flames stopped moving. The falling debris hung in the air. The boy's hand in his… went still. Daniel's heart dropped. "No… not now…" The world had paused again. And the clock appeared. Right in front of him. Burning… but untouched by the fire. Tick. Tick. A message formed across its cracked glass: "Interference detected." "Timeline deviation: unauthorized."

Daniel stepped back. "I was trying to help!" The ticking grew louder. Sharper. Almost angry. "Return immediately." The boy's frozen face stared up at him. So close to being saved. Daniel clenched his jaw. "No…" But the clock didn't wait.

The world snapped. Daniel collapsed onto his apartment floor, gasping violently. His chest burned. His throat felt raw from smoke that shouldn't exist anymore. He coughed hard, trying to breathe. Then he noticed something. His hand. Burn marks. Real. Painful. "This… this is real…"

His phone buzzed again. New message. "You broke the rule." Another one followed. "You are not there to save them." "You are there to observe." Daniel stared at the screen, anger rising in his chest. "Then why send me there?" he whispered. The reply came instantly. "To learn."

Daniel's grip tightened around the phone. Another message appeared. "Next jump in 00:59." A countdown began. 59… 58… 57… Daniel's breathing slowed. But his eyes hardened. "This time…" he said quietly, "I'm not just watching." The countdown continued.
